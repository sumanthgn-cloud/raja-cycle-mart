DIY Puncture Repair Guide

Published: November 2025 | Reading time: 7 minutes

A flat tyre doesn't have to mean a trip to the repair shop. With basic tools and this step-by-step guide, you can fix a puncture at home in about 30 minutes.

Tools You'll Need

  • Tyre levers (2-3 pieces)
  • Puncture patch kit
  • Sandpaper or scraper
  • Glue (from patch kit)
  • Bowl of water (to find the leak)
  • Bicycle pump

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step 1: Remove the Wheel

Release the brake (if needed), then remove the wheel. For rear wheels, shift to the smallest gear first. Use quick-release levers or a wrench to remove the wheel.

Step 2: Remove the Tyre

Deflate the tube completely. Insert one tyre lever under the tyre bead and hook it to a spoke. Insert a second lever a few inches away and work your way around to remove one side of the tyre.

Step 3: Find the Leak

Inflate the tube slightly and submerge it in water. Look for bubbles - that's where the puncture is. Mark the spot with a pen or chalk.

Step 4: Prepare and Patch

Dry the tube and roughen the area around the puncture with sandpaper. Apply glue in a thin layer, wait 2-3 minutes until tacky, then press the patch firmly. Hold for 30 seconds.

Step 5: Reinstall

Check the inside of the tyre for any sharp objects. Partially inflate the tube, place it back in the tyre, and work the tyre back onto the rim. Inflate to proper pressure.
