10 Essential Cycle Maintenance Tips Every Rider in Tumkur Should Know

Riding in Tumkur — with short city trips, occasional dirt tracks, and monsoon rains — means your bike needs regular care. These ten simple tips keep your cycle safe, reliable, and comfortable. They are easy, inexpensive, and perfect for busy riders who want long-lasting performance.

1. Check tyre pressure weekly

Underinflated tyres increase puncture risk and make pedaling harder. Use a pressure gauge; most city tyres prefer 40–65 PSI. Not sure? Ride to Raja Cycle Mart and we’ll set the right PSI for your model.

2. Inspect tyre tread and sidewalls

Before every ride, look for sharp glass, cuts, or bulges. Replace tyres that show deep cuts or exposed cords. Worn tread reduces grip, especially on wet roads.

3. Keep the chain clean and lubricated

Dirty chains wear faster and shift poorly. Wipe the chain with a rag, use degreaser when needed, and apply chain lube sparingly. Remove extra lube so dust doesn’t stick.

4. Check brakes every month

Make sure brake pads have enough material and are aligned with the rim. If braking feels spongy, tighten cables or bleed hydraulic lines. Reliable brakes are critical in Tumkur traffic.

5. True wheels and tighten spokes

Wobbly wheels rub brakes and wear tyres unevenly. A short truing session at Raja Cycle Mart restores smooth rolling and saves money long term.

6. Keep bolts and quick releases tight

Loose handlebars, saddles, or wheel releases are safety risks. Check them monthly with a multi-tool and tighten to manufacturer specs.

7. Inspect cables and housings

Frayed cables give delayed braking or shifting. If controls feel sticky, replace cables and housings. It’s a quick, cheap upgrade that feels amazing.

8. Protect from the monsoon

During rainy months, dry and relube components often, avoid parking in puddles, and use rust-protect spray on exposed metal parts.

9. Clean and store properly

After cleaning, let your bike dry and apply fresh lubrication. Store bikes under shade to avoid sun and moisture damage.

10. Regular tune-ups save money

A professional tune every 3–6 months prevents costly repairs. Raja Cycle Mart offers honest tune-ups, and our technician Mr. Shaber recommends only what’s needed.
